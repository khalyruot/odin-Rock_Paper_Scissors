Rock-paper-scissors is a simple hand game usually played between two people. The game has three possible outcomes other than a tie: rock beats scissors, scissors beats paper, and paper beats rock.
    Gameplay:
  • Both players count to three in unison (either audibly or in their heads).
  • On the count of three, each player simultaneously forms one of the three hand gestures (rock, paper, or scissors).
  • The players reveal their chosen gestures to each other.
  • Determine the winner based on the outcomes mentioned earlier: rock, paper, or scissors.
    Outcomes:
  • Player 1 chooses rock, and Player 2 chooses scissors. Player 1 wins.
  • Player 1 chooses rock, and Player 2 chooses scissors. Player 1 wins.
  • Player 1 chooses scissors, and Player 2 chooses paper. Player 1 wins.
  • Player 1 and Player 2 both choose the same gesture (e.g., both choose rock). The game is a tie.
